First Aid Guide
Step-by-step emergency instructions
✕
🚨
Call 108 immediately. Start CPR only if the person is unresponsive and not breathing normally.
1
Check Responsiveness
Tap shoulders firmly and shout "Are you okay?". Look for breathing — watch for chest rise for no more than 10 seconds.
⚠️ Do not move if spinal injury suspected
2
Call for Help
Shout for someone to call 108. Put phone on speaker so you can continue CPR hands-free while waiting for help.
3
Chest Compressions
Place heel of hand on centre of chest. Push down 5–6 cm at 100–120 beats per minute. Allow full chest recoil between each compression.
💡 Hum "Stayin' Alive" to keep the correct rhythm
4
Rescue Breaths (if trained)
After 30 compressions, tilt head back, lift chin. Pinch nose and give 2 breath of 1 second each until chest rises.
✓ Compression-only CPR is effective if untrained
5
Continue Until Help Arrives
Keep the 30:2 cycle going. Switch with another person every 2 minutes to avoid fatigue if someone else is available.
🤧
Ask "Are you choking?" — if they can cough forcefully, encourage coughing. If silent, act immediately.
1
Encourage Coughing
If the person can cough, speak, or cry — encourage them to cough hard. Stand nearby but do not interfere.
2
5 Back Blows
Lean them forward. Use the heel of your hand to give 5 firm blows between the shoulder blades.
⚠️ For infants — use 2 fingers, not heel of hand
3
Heimlich Manoeuvre
Stand behind them. One fist above navel, grasp with other hand. Give 5 firm upward abdominal thrusts.
4
Repeat Until Clear
Alternate 5 back blows and 5 abdominal thrusts. If they become unconscious, begin CPR and call 108.
💡 For pregnant persons — thrust on chest, not abdomen
🩸
For severe bleeding — act fast. Every second counts. Keep the person calm and still.
1
Protect Yourself
Use gloves or a plastic bag if available. Avoid direct contact with the blood whenever possible.
2
Apply Direct Pressure
Press firmly on the wound with a clean cloth, towel, or clothing. Maintain constant, firm pressure.
⚠️ Don't remove cloth — add more on top if soaked
3
Elevate if Possible
Raise the wounded limb above heart level to slow blood flow — only if no fracture is suspected.
4
Tourniquet for Limb Bleeds
If bleeding is uncontrolled on an arm or leg, apply tourniquet 5 cm above wound. Note the time of application.
💡 Mark "T" and time on forehead with a marker
5
Watch for Shock
Cover with a blanket. Watch for: pale/cold/clammy skin, rapid weak pulse, confusion, or loss of consciousness.
✓ Keep talking to keep them calm and conscious
🧠
Use the FAST test — Face, Arms, Speech, Time. Brain cells die every minute — act immediately.
F
Face Drooping
Ask to smile. Is one side drooping or numb? Is the smile uneven? Any facial numbness?
A
Arm Weakness
Ask to raise both arms. Does one drift downward? Is one arm weak, numb, or unable to rise?
S
Speech Difficulty
Ask a simple sentence. Is speech slurred, strange, or are they unable to speak or understand?
T
Time to Call 108
If ANY of the above — call 108 immediately. Note the exact time symptoms started. Keep patient lying, no food or water.
⚠️ Do NOT give aspirin unless directed by a doctor
🔥
Cool the burn — never apply ice, butter, or toothpaste. Seek help for burns larger than your palm.
1
Remove from Source
Move person away from heat, flames, or chemicals safely. Remove burning clothing — unless stuck to skin.
2
Cool with Running Water
Run cool (not cold) water over the burn for 10–20 minutes. Do NOT use ice — it causes further tissue damage.
⚠️ Never apply butter, oil, or toothpaste to burns
3
Cover Loosely
Cover with a clean non-fluffy material like cling film or a clean plastic bag. Don't wrap tightly.
4
Pain Relief & Hydration
Give paracetamol if available and they're conscious. Encourage drinking water for large area burns.
✓ Keep them warm — large burns cause rapid heat loss
🦴
Do not try to straighten a broken bone. Immobilise, support, and wait for professional help.
1
Stop Movement
Keep the injured area completely still. Ask the person to support the limb themselves if possible. Keep them calm.
2
Immobilise the Limb
Splint using a rolled newspaper, umbrella, or straight stick padded with soft material for comfort.
⚠️ Do NOT try to realign bones — for doctors only
3
Control Bleeding (Open Fracture)
If bone is visible, cover with a sterile dressing. Apply gentle pressure around, not directly on, the wound.
4
Ice & Elevate
Wrap an ice pack in cloth and apply to reduce swelling. Elevate the limb above heart level if possible.
✓ Press nail until white then release — should pink in 2 sec (circulation check)
